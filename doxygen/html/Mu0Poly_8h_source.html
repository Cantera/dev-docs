18 //! The Mu0Poly class implements an interpolation of the Gibbs free energy based
19 //! on a piecewise constant heat capacity approximation.
20 /*!
21  * The Mu0Poly class implements a piecewise constant heat capacity
22  * approximation. of the standard state chemical potential of one species at a
23  * single reference pressure. The chemical potential is input as a series of
24  * (\f$T\f$, \f$ \mu^o(T)\f$) values. The first temperature is assumed to be
25  * equal to 298.15 K; however, this may be relaxed in the future. This
26  * information, and an assumption of a constant heat capacity within each
27  * interval is enough to calculate all thermodynamic functions.
28  *
29  * The piece-wise constant heat capacity is calculated from the change in the
30  * chemical potential over each interval. Once the heat capacity is known, the
31  * other thermodynamic functions may be determined. The basic equation for going
32  * from temperature point 1 to temperature point 2 are as follows for \f$ T \f$,
33  * \f$ T_1 <= T <= T_2 \f$
34  *
35  * \f[
36  * \mu^o(T_1) = h^o(T_1) - T_1 * s^o(T_1)
37  * \f]
38  * \f[
39  * \mu^o(T_2) - \mu^o(T_1) = Cp^o(T_1)(T_2 - T_1) - Cp^o(T_1)(T_2)ln(\frac{T_2}{T_1}) - s^o(T_1)(T_2 - T_1)
40  * \f]
41  * \f[
42  * s^o(T_2) = s^o(T_1) + Cp^o(T_1)ln(\frac{T_2}{T_1})
43  * \f]
44  * \f[
45  * h^o(T_2) = h^o(T_1) + Cp^o(T_1)(T_2 - T_1)
46  * \f]
47  *
48  * Within each interval the following relations are used. For \f$ T \f$, \f$
49  * T_1 <= T <= T_2 \f$
50  *
51  * \f[
52  * \mu^o(T) = \mu^o(T_1) + Cp^o(T_1)(T - T_1) - Cp^o(T_1)(T_2)ln(\frac{T}{T_1}) - s^o(T_1)(T - T_1)
53  * \f]
54  * \f[
55  * s^o(T) = s^o(T_1) + Cp^o(T_1)ln(\frac{T}{T_1})
56  * \f]
57  * \f[
58  * h^o(T) = h^o(T_1) + Cp^o(T_1)(T - T_1)
59  * \f]
60  *
61  * Notes about temperature interpolation for \f$ T < T_1 \f$ and \f$ T >
62  * T_{npoints} \f$: These are achieved by assuming a constant heat capacity
63  * equal to the value in the closest temperature interval. No error is thrown.
64  *
65  * @note In the future, a better assumption about the heat capacity may be
66  * employed, so that it can be continuous.
67  *
68  * @ingroup spthermo
69  */
