22 /*!
23  * GibbsExcessVPSSTP is a derived class of ThermoPhase that handles variable
24  * pressure standard state methods for calculating thermodynamic properties that
25  * are further based on expressing the Excess Gibbs free energy as a function of
26  * the mole fractions (or pseudo mole fractions) of constituents. This category
27  * is the workhorse for describing molten salts, solid-phase mixtures of
28  * semiconductors, and mixtures of miscible and semi-miscible compounds.
29  *
30  * It includes
31  * - regular solutions
32  * - Margules expansions
33  * - NTRL equation
34  * - Wilson's equation
35  * - UNIQUAC equation of state.
36  *
37  * This class adds additional functions onto the ThermoPhase interface that
38  * handles the calculation of the excess Gibbs free energy. The ThermoPhase
39  * class includes a member function, ThermoPhase::activityConvention() that
40  * indicates which convention the activities are based on. The default is to
41  * assume activities are based on the molar convention. That default is used
42  * here.
43  *
44  * All of the Excess Gibbs free energy formulations in this area employ
45  * symmetrical formulations.
46  *
47  * Chemical potentials of species k, \f$ \mu_o \f$, has the following general
48  * format:
49  *
50  * \f[
51  * \mu_k = \mu^o_k(T,P) + R T ln( \gamma_k X_k )
52  * \f]
53  *
54  * where \f$ \gamma_k^{\triangle} \f$ is a molar based activity coefficient for
55  * species \f$k\f$.
56  *
57  * GibbsExcessVPSSTP contains an internal vector with the current mole fraction
58  * vector. That's one of its primary usages. In order to keep the mole fraction
59  * vector constant, all of the setState functions are redesigned at this layer.
60  *
61  * <H3>
62  * Activity Concentrations: Relationship of ThermoPhase to %Kinetics Expressions
63  * </H3>
64  *
65  * As explained in a similar discussion in the ThermoPhase class, the actual
66  * units used in kinetics expressions must be specified in the ThermoPhase class
67  * for the corresponding species. These units vary with the field of study.
68  * %Cantera uses the concept of activity concentrations to represent this.
69  * Activity concentrations are used directly in the expressions for kinetics.
70  * Standard concentrations are used as the multiplicative constant that takes
71  * the activity of a species and turns it into an activity concentration.
72  * Standard concentrations must not depend on the concentration of the species
73  * in the phase.
74  *
75  * Here we set a standard for the specification of the standard concentrations
76  * for this class and all child classes underneath it. We specify here that the
77  * standard concentration is equal to 1 for all species. Therefore, the
78  * activities appear directly in kinetics expressions involving species in
79  * underlying GibbsExcessVPSSTP phases.
80  *
81  * <H3>
82  * SetState Strategy
83  * </H3>
84  *
85  * All setState functions that set the internal state of the ThermoPhase object
86  * are overloaded at this level, so that a current mole fraction vector is
87  * maintained within the object.
88  */

107  /**
108  * Calculate the density of the mixture using the partial molar volumes and
109  * mole fractions as input
110  *
111  * The formula for this is
112  *
113  * \f[
114  * \rho = \frac{\sum_k{X_k W_k}}{\sum_k{X_k V_k}}
115  * \f]
116  *
117  * where \f$X_k\f$ are the mole fractions, \f$W_k\f$ are the molecular
118  * weights, and \f$V_k\f$ are the pure species molar volumes.
119  *
120  * Note, the basis behind this formula is that in an ideal solution the
121  * partial molar volumes are equal to the pure species molar volumes. We
122  * have additionally specified in this class that the pure species molar
123  * volumes are independent of temperature and pressure.
124  *
125  * NOTE: This is a non-virtual function, which is not a member of the
126  * ThermoPhase base class.
