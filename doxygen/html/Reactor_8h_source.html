14 /**
15  * Class Reactor is a general-purpose class for stirred reactors. The reactor
16  * may have an arbitrary number of inlets and outlets, each of which may be
17  * connected to a "flow device" such as a mass flow controller, a pressure
18  * regulator, etc. Additional reactors may be connected to the other end of
19  * the flow device, allowing construction of arbitrary reactor networks.
20  *
21  * The reactor class integrates the same governing equations no matter what
22  * type of reactor is simulated. The differences among reactor types are
23  * completely specified by the attached flow devices and the time-dependent
24  * user-specified boundary conditions.
25  *
26  * If an instance of class Reactor is used directly, it will simulate an
27  * adiabatic, constant volume reactor with gas-phase chemistry but no surface
28  * chemistry. Other reactor types may be simulated by deriving a class from
29  * Reactor. This method allows specifying the following in terms of the
30  * instantaneous reactor state:
31  *
32  * - rate of change of the total volume (m^3/s)
33  * - surface heat loss rate (W)
34  * - species surface production rates (kmol/s)
35  */
