17 //! Class SimpleTransport implements mixture-averaged transport
18 //! properties for liquid phases.
19 /*!
20  * The model is based on that described by Newman, Electrochemical Systems
21  *
22  * The velocity of species i may be described by the
23  * following equation p. 297 (12.1)
24  *
25  * \f[
26  * c_i \nabla \mu_i = R T \sum_j \frac{c_i c_j}{c_T D_{ij}}
27  * (\mathbf{v}_j - \mathbf{v}_i)
28  * \f]
29  *
30  * This as written is degenerate by 1 dof.
31  *
32  * To fix this we must add in the definition of the mass averaged
33  * velocity of the solution. We will call the simple bold-faced
34  * \f$\mathbf{v} \f$
35  * symbol the mass-averaged velocity. Then, the relation
36  * between \f$\mathbf{v}\f$ and the individual species velocities is
37  * \f$\mathbf{v}_i\f$
38  *
39  * \f[
40  * \rho_i \mathbf{v}_i = \rho_i \mathbf{v} + \mathbf{j}_i
41  * \f]
42  * where \f$\mathbf{j}_i\f$ are the diffusional fluxes of species i
43  * with respect to the mass averaged velocity and
44  *
45  * \f[
46  * \sum_i \mathbf{j}_i = 0
47  * \f]
48  *
49  * and
50  *
51  * \f[
52  * \sum_i \rho_i \mathbf{v}_i = \rho \mathbf{v}
53  * \f]
54  *
55  * Using these definitions, we can write
56  *
57  * \f[
58  * \mathbf{v}_i = \mathbf{v} + \frac{\mathbf{j}_i}{\rho_i}
59  * \f]
60  *
61  * \f[
62  * c_i \nabla \mu_i = R T \sum_j \frac{c_i c_j}{c_T D_{ij}}
63  * (\frac{\mathbf{j}_j}{\rho_j} - \frac{\mathbf{j}_i}{\rho_i})
64  * = R T \sum_j \frac{1}{D_{ij}}
65  * (\frac{x_i \mathbf{j}_j}{M_j} - \frac{x_j \mathbf{j}_i}{M_i})
66  * \f]
67  *
68  * The equations that we actually solve are
69  *
70  * \f[
71  * c_i \nabla \mu_i =
72  * = R T \sum_j \frac{1}{D_{ij}}
73  * (\frac{x_i \mathbf{j}_j}{M_j} - \frac{x_j \mathbf{j}_i}{M_i})
74  * \f]
75  * and we replace the 0th equation with the following:
76  *
77  * \f[
78  * \sum_i \mathbf{j}_i = 0
79  * \f]
80  *
81  * When there are charged species, we replace the rhs with the
82  * gradient of the electrochemical potential to obtain the
83  * modified equation
84  *
85  * \f[
86  * c_i \nabla \mu_i + c_i F z_i \nabla \Phi
87  * = R T \sum_j \frac{1}{D_{ij}}
88  * (\frac{x_i \mathbf{j}_j}{M_j} - \frac{x_j \mathbf{j}_i}{M_i})
89  * \f]
90  *
91  * With this formulation we may solve for the diffusion velocities,
92  * without having to worry about what the mass averaged velocity
93  * is.
94  *
95  * <H2> Viscosity Calculation </H2>
96  *
97  * The viscosity calculation may be broken down into two parts.
98  * In the first part, the viscosity of the pure species are calculated
99  * In the second part, a mixing rule is applied. There are two mixing rules.
100  * Solvent-only and mixture-averaged.
101  *
102  * For the solvent-only mixing rule, we use the pure species viscosity calculated for
103  * the solvent as the viscosity of the entire mixture. For the mixture averaged rule
104  * we do a mole fraction based average of the pure species viscosities:
105  *
106  * Solvent-only:
107  * \f[
108  * \mu = \mu_0
109  * \f]
110  * Mixture-average:
111  * \f[
112  * \mu = \sum_k {\mu_k X_k}
113  * \f]
114  *
115  * <H2> Calculate of the Binary Diffusion Coefficients </H2>
116  *
117  * The binary diffusion coefficients are obtained from the pure species diffusion coefficients
118  * using an additive process
119  *
120  * \f[
121  * D_{i,j} = \frac{1}{2} \left( D^0_i(T) + D^0_j(T) \right)
122  * \f]
123  *
124  * <H2> Electrical Mobilities </H2>
125  *
126  * The mobility \f$ \mu^e_k \f$ is calculated from the diffusion coefficient using the Einstein relation.
127  *
128  * \f[
129  * \mu^e_k = \frac{F D_k}{R T}
130  * \f]
131  *
132  * The diffusion coefficients, \f$ D_k \f$ , is calculated from a call to the mixture diffusion
133  * coefficient routine.
134  *
135  * <H2> Species Diffusive Fluxes </H2>
136  *
137  * The diffusive mass flux of species \e k is computed from the following
138  * formula
139  *
140  * Usually the specified solution average velocity is the mass averaged velocity.
141  * This is changed in some subclasses, however.
142  *
143  * \f[
144  * j_k = - c^T M_k D_k \nabla X_k - \rho Y_k V_c
145  * \f]
146  *
147  * where V_c is the correction velocity
148  *
149  * \f[
150  * \rho V_c = - \sum_j {c^T M_j D_j \nabla X_j}
151  * \f]
152  *
153  * In the above equation, \f$ D_k \f$ is the mixture diffusivity for species k calculated for the current
154  * conditions, which may depend on T, P, and X_k. \f$ C^T \f$ is the total concentration of the phase.
155  *
156  * When this is electrical migration, the formulas above are enhanced to
157  *
158  * \f[
159  * j_k = - C^T M_k D_k \nabla X_k + F C^T M_k \frac{D_k}{ R T } X_k z_k \nabla V - \rho Y_k V_c
160  * \f]
161  *
162  * where V_c is the correction velocity
163  *
164  * \f[
165  * \rho V_c = - \sum_j {c^T M_j D_j \nabla X_j} + \sum_j F C^T M_j \frac{D_j}{ R T } X_j z_j \nabla V
166  * \f]
167  *
168  * <H2> Species Diffusional Velocities </H2>
169  *
170  * Species diffusional velocities are calculated from the species diffusional fluxes, within this object,
171  * using the following formula for the diffusional velocity of the kth species, \f$ V_k^d \f$
172  *
173  * \f[
174  * j_k = \rho Y_k V_k^d
175  * \f]
176  *
177  * TODO
178  * This object has to be made compatible with different types of reference velocities. Right now, elements
179  * of the formulas are only compatible with the mass-averaged velocity.
180  *
181  * @ingroup tranprops
182  */
